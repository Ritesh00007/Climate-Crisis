Key Climate Facts

Six crucial facts about climate change

01

Rising Temperatures

The Earth's average temperature has increased by about 1.1°C since the pre-industrial era, primarily due to human activities.

02

CO₂ Levels

Carbon dioxide levels in the atmosphere are higher than they have been in 800,000 years, exceeding 420 parts per million in 2023.

03

Arctic Ice Loss

The Arctic is warming nearly twice as fast as the global average, with summer sea ice declining by approximately 13% per decade.

04

Sea Level Rise

Global sea levels have risen about 8-9 inches since 1880, with the rate accelerating to 3.7 mm per year in recent decades.

05

Extreme Weather

Climate change is increasing the frequency and intensity of extreme weather events, including heatwaves, droughts, and heavy rainfall.

06

Ocean Acidification

The ocean has absorbed about 30% of human-produced carbon dioxide, resulting in a 30% increase in ocean acidity since the Industrial Revolution.
